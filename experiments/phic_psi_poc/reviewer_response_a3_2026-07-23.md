## Addendum (same day) — two follow-up points

### 1. Final-stage mchirp degradation (t = +4.2 to +9.0) — now explained in §6.6

Your proposed mechanism (single-batch resumption overfitting) is confirmed from evidence already in hand, without a new run:

- the probe pipeline is stage-independent, yet the identical code yields strongly *negative* mchirp deltas early (t = −3.4 to −8.5) and *positive* ones late — a data-loading quirk cannot be training-stage-dependent;
- the gradient batch is disjoint from the probe by construction (samples 512–639 vs 0–511), so the degradation is genuine out-of-batch generalization loss, not leakage;
- the effect is confined to the head with a real strain-to-target mapping — a converged optimum exists to be dragged away from; the dead periodic heads show only sub-significant drifts.

Per your suggestion, §6.6 now claims it as an unplanned second control: the paired channel demonstrably detects a real (if artifactual) effect at the converged stage, and φc/ψ still show nothing against that more sensitive backdrop.
The script additionally now logs the batch total loss per step, so any future rerun captures the batch-falls-while-probe-rises divergence directly.

### 2. Minimum-detectable-effect note — added as polish

§6.6 now states: per-combo MDE (≈ 2× paired SE) spans ≈ 0.005–0.10 loss units, so the final-stage per-combo null is "not rejected and bounded at the few-percent level," not "excluded to arbitrary precision" — adequate for A.3's mechanistic role, with the degeneracy verdict carried independently by the N = 10,000 bootstrap and the SNR stratification, as you note.
